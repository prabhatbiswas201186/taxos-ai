# India Tax Regulations 2025 – Research Report

## Executive Summary

India is undergoing a major fiscal and structural transformation in 2025, driven by three interlocking forces:

1. **The Income Tax Act, 2025** – a complete statutory overhaul replacing the 1961 Act, effective 1 April 2026 (FY 2026-27 onward).
2. **Budget 2025 interim reliefs** – significant slab-rate reductions, altered TDS/TCS thresholds, and expanded Section 87A rebates effective 1 April 2025 (FY 2025-26).
3. **IFRS/SDRT and indirect-tax reforms** – continued rollout of e-invoicing, GST compliance automation, and data-center–specific tax holidays.

This report summarizes the most operationally relevant items for a tax-AI / fintech business targeting the Indian market.

---

## 1. Income-Tax Act, 2025 (New Act)

### 1.1 Timeline and Structure

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Introduced as | Income-Tax (No. 2) Bill, 2025 (passed Monsoon Session 2025) |
| Effective | 1 April 2026 (FY 2026-27 onwards) |
| Replaces | Income-tax Act, 1961 (819 sections → 536 sections, same 23 chapters) |
| Core promise | Simplification, no rate changes, no major policy changes |
| Governing body | Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT); Income-tax Rules, 2025 notified |

### 1.2 Core Conceptual Changes

- **Unified terminology**: "Tax Year" replaces the dual "Assessment Year" / "Previous Year" construct.
- **Preserved continuity**: Carry-forward losses, unabsorbed depreciation, tax credits, and pre-2026 years remain governed by the Erstwhile Act.
- **VDA definition**: Virtual Digital Assets (cryptocurrencies, tokenized assets) are formally defined for the first time.

### 1.3 Tax Planning Opportunities

- **Sections 80-IAC startups**: 100% profit deduction for 3 years out of 10 if incorporated before 1 April 2030.
- **IFSC incentives**: Sunset for IFSC operational-unit concessions extended to 31 March 2030; life-insurance premiums paid by non-residents to IFSC offices are fully exempt under Section 10(10D) with no cap.
- **Lower MAT regime**: Finance Act 2026 reduced MAT rates and overhauled MAT credit utilization.

## 2. FY 2025-26 Interim Measures (Effective 1 April 2025)

These changes apply before the full New Act comes into force and frame the immediate compliance landscape.

### 2.1 New Tax Regime Slabs (Default)

| Income Slab | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Up to Rs. 4 lakh | Nil |
| Rs. 4 lakh – Rs. 8 lakh | 5% |
| Rs. 8 lakh – Rs. 12 lakh | 10% |
| Rs. 12 lakh – Rs. 16 lakh | 15% |
| Rs. 16 lakh – Rs. 20 lakh | 20% |
| Rs. 20 lakh – Rs. 24 lakh | 25% |
| Above Rs. 24 lakh | 30% |

- **Section 87A rebate** increased to Rs. 60,000 for New Regime filers → effective tax-free limit up to Rs. 12 lakh.

### 2.2 Enhanced TDS Thresholds

Major brackets raised across sections 193, 194, 194A, 194J, 194I, 194H, 194LA, 194D, 194G, 194K, 194B/BB.

- Rent threshold shifted from annual Rs. 2.4 lakh to monthly Rs. 50,000.
- Professional/technical service fees threshold raised from Rs. 30,000 to Rs. 50,000.
- Insurance commission, lottery ticket commission/prize all raised to Rs. 20,000.

### 2.3 TCS Relaxations

- LRS remittance threshold raised from Rs. 7 lakh to Rs. 10 lakh.
- Education LRS financed via educational loan: **No TCS**.
- Purchase of goods TCS (206C(1H)): threshold earlier at Rs. 50 lakh now **omitted**.

### 2.4 ITR-U Extended Window

- Updated return (ITR-U) filing window increased from 12 months to **48 months** from end of relevant AY.
- Additional tax penalty schedule: 25% (within 12 mths) → 50% (within 24 mths) → 60% (within 36 mths) → 70% (within 48 mths).

## 3. GST and Indirect Tax Updates

### 3.1 Ongoing E-Invoicing Rollout

- Mandatory e-invoicing thresholds have remained stable, with continued API-integration expectations from the GSTN.
- Real-time reporting elevates tax-tech product quality bar: tolerance for latency and reconciliation error is near zero.

### 3.2 GST Notifications (2025)

- CBIC circulars in early and mid-2025 clarified refund rules, e-waybill integrations, and input-tax-credit (ITC) matching mechanisms.
- Refund eligibility restrictions clarified: no refund for tax, interest, or penalty already discharged for the entire prior period (gst-ct-11-2025).

## 4. Compliance Burden and Penalty Rationalisation (Finance Act 2026)

| Penalty Category | Change |
|------------------|--------|
| Failure to produce books | Decriminalised |
| TDS on in-kind transfers | Decriminalised |
| Tax default ≤ INR 10 lakh | Decriminalised |
| Delayed audit reports / TP docs | Fixed or daily fee caps |
| Misreporting of income | Expanded definition (unexplained credit, investment, asset, or expenditure) |
| Immunity provision | Additional tax of 100% (120% for unexplained credit/expenditure) grants immunity from penalty and prosecution |

## 5. Market Implications for Taxos AI

1. **Increased automation demand** – expanded ITR-U windows, lower TDS/TCS thresholds, and GST reconciliation rules all generate higher filing frequency and larger datasets per taxpayer.
2. **Simplified legislation creates training-data opportunity** – the Income-tax Act, 2025 reduces section count by ~35%; mapping 1961 provisions → 2025 provisions is a distinct content product.
3. **Startup tax holiday window (until 2030)** creates a discrete cohort eligible for 80-IAC benefits, worth targeting directly with AI-driven Form 10IC and verification automation.
4. **Data-center / IFSC exemptions** generate non-resident filing scenarios that benefit from multi-jurisdiction AI research.
